Overview


The 90s Virtual Museum: A Filipino Journey is more than just a museum; it's a portal to a bygone era that shaped the Filipino identity. Whether you're a nostalgic Gen X'er or a curious millennial, our virtual museum invites you to relive the memories, discover the history, and immerse yourself in the vibrant culture that defined the 1990s in the Philippines.

Cultural Key Event

https://www.ateneo.edu/features/2022/05/27/institute-philippine-culture-through-years-transformation-adaptation-impact

In the 1990s, traditional cultural practices and festivals continued to be an integral part of Filipino life. Festivals like Sinulog, Panagbenga, and Pahiyas were celebrated with great enthusiasm, showcasing local arts, music, dance, and religious traditions.

Economy Key Event

https://www.elibrary.imf.org/display/book/9781557758613/ch05.xml

The Philippines emphasized export-oriented industrialization during the 1990s. Garments, electronics, and other manufactured goods were among the primary exports.

Environmental Key Event

https://www.statista.com/topics/5845/natural-disasters-in-the-philippines-at-a-glance/#topicOverview

The Philippines, being prone to natural disasters, faced challenges in managing the aftermath of typhoons, earthquakes, and volcanic eruptions.

Political Key Event

https://www.womenshistory.org/education-resources/biographies/corazon-aquino

The Philippines continued to recover from the dictatorship of Ferdinand Marcos, who was ousted in the People Power Revolution of 1986. Corazon Aquino served as the President during the late 1980s and early 1990s, overseeing the transition to democracy.

Social Key Event

https://www.queensjournal.ca/then-and-now-communication-through-the-ages/

Primarily reliant on landline phones and written letters. Face-to-face interactions and traditional media like newspapers and television were the main sources of news and information.

Political

https://pdp.neda.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/Chapter-01.pdf

The Philippine government has set ambitious targets for the country’s development in the coming years. The Philippine Development Plan (PDP) 2023-2028 is a plan for deep economic and social transformation to reinvigorate job creation and accelerate poverty reduction by steering the economy back on a high-growth path. The plan aims to achieve a $1.5-trillion economy by 2030, with a per capita income of $10,000.
